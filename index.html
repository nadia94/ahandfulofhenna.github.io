Hi, I'm Nadia , a mehndi artist.


I’ve been curating a few stories recently and would love to share them with you, so thanks for stopping by.
My journey started as a creative project for my Honours in Media Theory and Practice at UCT. I have used my growing skill in mehndi design to weave the stories of 8 people from very different walks of life into motifs and symbols that represent their special love stories. These are intricately traced among those characteristic of the traditional styles. Mehndi has traditionally been used for symbolising the love between a bride and groom on their wedding day. The ceremonial aspect behind this is what made me choose to experiment with mehndi as a narrative art form. Below you will find the stories along with explanations of the process.

Nadia - Don't forget to fall in love with yourself first

From my early childhood days, I can recall having mehndi applied to my hands and nails during a wedding period and also for Eid. Still having small hands, which meant not a lot of space to draw, my grandmother would apply what was known in our specific Indian culture as “lapeto”. It was applying mehndi in a solid layer, to the inner part of your hand, from the tips of the fingers to the bottom of the palms. This would result in a deep orange stain, that was simple and effortless. We had a mehndi tree that grew in the front garden, so my grandmother would “pilaar” her own mehndi whenever we needed. She would begin the process by drying the leaves for a few days, until they were crispy and could easily be crushed. Once they were ready, she would boil a mug of strong black tea and pour it into the mix of crushed mehndi leaves. A paste would then form. The paste would have to sit over a night or two, to soak up the tea and activate the leaves. The next step was to sift the paste through a fine cloth to get rid of the stalks and anything that would not ensure a smooth finish.

As I grew older, I wanted a more sophisticated hand of mehndi. My grandmother may have known how to make the mehndi, but try as we might, we could never get the perfect cone. And so piping out patterns onto my hand was not in the cards until much later on when they would come directly from India. So without the cone, my grandmother would take a needle, dip it in the paste and deposit it bit by bit onto my hand, each addition adding to the previous one, to form the shape of a flower. As she grew older, her patience would wear thin and it was then up to me to draw my own flowers. But the passion didn’t last very long and I grew out of my love for mehndi. After some years, I found an old cone in the fridge and picked up the tradition once again. I began drawing flowers and circles and spirals and leaves down my hand and the fascination built up inside me. I would Google patterns to copy from, and practice on my left hand, eagerly waiting for the previous stain to fade so that I could replace it with a different design. As I got more and more accustomed to the design process and application, I became quicker and more creative. That's when I took the art of mehndi more seriously. I decided to use this opportunity to flex my creativity and challenge myself artistically and hence I began this project of usign mehndi as a way to tell stories.

When I began this project, I knew I wanted to centre it the stories on love because one of the most prominent times a woman gets her mehdni done is for her wedding day. This made me I think about romantic love and so I thought about asking people to share their romantic experiences with me.
But then I realised that love doesn’t fit into a box; there are so many other wonderful kinds of love that exist.

This is how I came to choose my own story, which is about self-love.

For the past 4 years I have lived by myself, having moved away from home to study, and through that experience I have learnt what it truly is to appreciate your own company and independence. I may have had to grow up quicker than some of my friends and learn to be respnosible in more ways than they did, but even though it was tough, the result is a rewarding one. I have learnt that you may be walking in a different direction to everyone else, but as long as you are happy with where you are going, and the person you are while taking that journey, you will be more than OK.

To start my story, I included a heart on my pinky finger which represents the love that I have for myself. The pattern flows down my hand
into a display of a house, the UCT logo and a few dollar signs symbolising money. These motifs express my journey from home to living in Cape Town and studying at UCT. The life lessons that I learnt along the way and the financial responsibilities, as well as other responsibilities of being an adult and taking care of myself, that I took on are represented in these as well. I ended the design with flowers as a reminder of all the beautiful things that I have in my life, from friendships to health to opportunities of success. And all of these things could not be be possible without me having taken my journey of self-love.

Garance - A Journey of Love

Standing at the top of Lions Head, Garance took in the staggering scene of the city of Cape Town below her and fell in love. With the breath-taking Table Mountain to her left, the sparkling and calming ocean to her right, and the millions of amazing people, places and activities to do, she couldn't help but feel her heart longing. Coming from France, she was only in Cape Town for two weeks to visit a friend, and she already knew that those two weeks would never be enough for her. She would continue to lust after the Mother City until she could call it her second home. She glanced over the view again and still overwhelmed by her love for this extraordinary place, she took her vows then and there. She made a promise to herself that she would come back one day and live here. A little while after that trip, Garance was offered the opportunity her heart had been waiting for. She was back in Cape Town within a heart beat, fulfilling her dreams. She gets to work as a writer during the week and as an organiser at weddings over the weekends. She spends her time indulging in every aspect and opportunity that Cape Town has to offer, from attending parties at Shimmy Beach Club, to hiking the mountains and swimming the seas, and sipping her favourite wines in the Cape Winelands. With the honeymoon phase of the relationship still intoxicating her, Garance likes to call Paris her wife, but Cape Town her lover.

To depict Garance's epic story, I began with drawing the lotus flower to represent her. It is placed at the centre of her wrist, with the rest of the motifs surrounding it. She chose the lotus flower because she likes to live her life with balance, zen and always puts her health first. This was made obvious to me when she asked, multiple times, if the mehndi was natural and not filled with chemicals, because she would not want to get anything harmful into her system. The lotus flower is enveloped with Table Mountain and Lions Head on the one side, and the Eiffel Tower on the other, representing her relationship with both Cape Town and Paris.

The word L'amour, which means love, has sentimental value to her as it is a name of a street in Paris. There is also a wine glass for the weddings she works at on the wine farms. Garance has come a long way to find love, but now that she has, she can add falling in love with a place to her list of different love stories.

Zannon -The Love from a Friendship is Beautiful

Zannon has always lived his life with the ethos of choosing love. His advice is that whenever you are faced with any decisions in life, if you put love first then you will invariably be making the right decision. With this in mind, Zannon relates his story about falling in love which centres around platonic a platonic relationship. He describes the mutual feelings that him and his friend share as being beautiful.

His story began three or four months ago when he met a straight guy and they quickly became friends. They shared a love for each other that was based purely on the the character, personality and attributes that they gave to each other and to those around them. Zannon thinks of his freindship as a basin of love because even though it is not romantic, and is not based on sexual attraction, it is something that is so special to him because he feels that it is all about one falling in love with the other person’s inner self and not just their appearance. There is more appreciation for what they bring into the relationship. He can honestly say that he loves his friend and that he is sure his friend can say the same thing about him.

There may be different kinds of love that everyone experiences; love towards a friend, love towards a family member, loves towards that special someone and whatever it may be, love will always assume a big role in his life.

In his mehndi design, I incorporated a ying-yang sign that represents for him the two fold that love holds, the give and take that needs each relationship should have to succeed. To have the right concept of what love is, and to experience its true greatness, there needs to be a balance of giving and receiving. I also used the symbol for friendship as a way of showing the love he has for his friend. Because this plays a big role in his relationship, I have featured it twice.

Iris - Love doesn’t die

Born and bred in Mexico, Iris had the dreams of travelling and writing. She studied animation but wanted to share her passion and skill with the rest of the world. In the middle of 2017, the doors of opportunity opened for her in the form of a writing internship in Cape Town, and she ran to them immediately. But as always, when one door opens, it means you have to close one door behind you, and for Iris, it was the door to her relationship with her boyfriend of six and a half years. They had both decided that moving away from home, it would be best to take a break from their relationship.

Having been away from Mexico for five months now, Iris and her boyfriend still share a love for each other. It may not be a romantic one anymore, but they are still there for each other when they need to be. They continue to care and support each other, demonstrating that love isn’t something that just dies. It doesn’t disappear into the distance that may float between the two hearts. She shared this sentiment when she mentioned that her ex had a bouquet of proteas delivered to her office on her first day there, to commemorate Iris on living out her dreams. They still spend hours talking on the phone, sharing all the details of the current events in their respective continents. phone and updating each other on current events in their separate continents. And whatever it comes down to, they always wish each other the best of everything. “He was the guy I could imagine marrying, but even if he ended up with another girl and marrying her, I would honestly wish him the best. All I want for him is for him to be happy.”

Iris's design includes lots of flowers that centre around the happiness and tenderness that is still evident in her relationship with her ex. These flowers relate to the happiness that both of them want for each other. The pattern then progresses to little motifs that encircle one of the flowers on the back of her palm. A house and a protea are enveloped by the South African flag and the Mexican flag, a tribute to her journey. The house represents the journey Iris took in leaving her home in Mexico and coming to live here in Cape Town while the protea is for the bouquet she received from her ex on her first day of work. The fingers end off the design and conclude her story with a few traditional motifs

Leandre - Faithful love

The Having sat down with Leandre, she reminded me of another love that is often excluded from the conversation, and that is the love one has for their religion and God. Her eyes sparkling and her smile widening, she described what love meant to her as the unconditional and overwhelming feeling you get inside of you when you possess a love for someone or sometihng. It needn't matter if it be intangible or physically invisible, your love can still be strong.

For Leandre, personally, it's about giving love to your family, friends and those closest to you without holding back. She emphasises about creating and sharing happiness with the ones you love, because at the end of it all, everyone wants to, and deserves to be happy. But the biggest part of sharing love for her encompasses God and having faith. As a firm believer in Christianity, she says that her values are instilled in her and that is why her love for her beliefs is so important to her.

In her mehndi design, I incorporated an anchor as an allegory for the role her parents play in her life. The peace sign and tree of life are demonstrative of the balance and positive energies that she focuses on to keep her life and love grounded. I also integrated a cross because her religion and beliefs play a big role in everything she does, as well as her love for God being so great. included a heart that covers quite a bit of her hand because I wanted it to represent the idea that for her, love means everything, so it symbolises the magnitude that it plays in her life.

Shauntel - Sailing away on a love boat

A hiking lover and outdoorsy person at heart, it is no wonder that Shauntel met the love of her life on a weekend of adventure and sport. She was introduced to her now fiancé through a mutual hobby of sailing. Their story began when she walked into the room, making eye contact with Michael and feeling an instant connection. There were lots of smiles going around while they sat and played coy, flirting over a cup of coffee, something which Michael never indulges in except he did on that day. The two days passed in so much entrancement that Shauntel had forgotten to give Michael her number. After having to jump through a few hoops with their captains to try and organise contact details they were finally on the right path.

I began Shauntel's desgin with a few sail boats sailing down towards her wrist. Because this was a major aspect of her story it took up a large portion of her design. The flowers represent the good and beautiful things in her life, including her relationship with her finace. On each finger, I drew a motif. The pinky finger has a coffee cup, because on the day she met Michael, he was sipping on coffee, something he never has. The ring finger has a game controller which represents Michael himself and it was strategically placed on this finger to further symbolise their union. The middle finger and index finger have an ice cream cone and putt putt respectively to dictate how they spent their Friday nights getting to know each other.

Shamema - Family is worth every tear

A mother to two beautiful daughters, Shamema has a single word that means everything to her, and that is family. Breaking down at the mere mention of them displays the depths her heart will go. As she wiped the tears off her cheeks and smiles while questioning why she always cries at moments like these, she describes the love between a parent and a child as being unconditional. And that is why her story revolves around sharing this bond. being so close to her daughters and sharing with them as they grow up is what keeps their love alive. There’s nothing she would have any other way.

In Shamema’s pattern, I weaved the mother and baby symbol to represent both her daughters in their mother’s embrace. I also used a heart with a smaller heart to demonstrate the love that she has for each daughter. Louise - A mother's love is all encompassing

Love is a very broad concept, as Louise found out over the years. She realises that it is not like she used to think it was, where it would just be about one person or nothing. Now, it means something that is in her heart, a feeling that makes it expand. Love can be towards a friend. It can be a relationship with her family or her partner. It can even be a beautiful sunset or someone who has just done something wonderful. The first thing that comes to her mind when speaking about love is the all encompassing love of a mother. Having lost hers when she was 9, her father remarried to, as Louise describes her, “the most wonderful, wonderful woman in the world.” And for Louise, she is everything that one could want in a mother or a friend. So for her, no matter where she is or what she is, how ever long she lives, this will be her lasting memory of someone who is absolutely amazing.

For Louise I drew a cat peeping out from surrounding flowers. She says that the cat well represents her mother seeing as she loves cats. I included it among the flowers to demonstrate the vastness of love that Louise and her mother share. And as with the other patterns, the flowers also represent everything that is beautiful in her life. I added a heart to the top of the design as a way of reiterating the love.
